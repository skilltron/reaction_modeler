## 5. B12 conversion (adenosyl and methyl)

*Two pathways: adeno = adenosylcobalamin (mitochondria, MMA); methyl = methylcobalamin (cytoplasm, homocysteine → methionine). An adeno conversion issue does not imply a methyl conversion issue.*

| Gene | Pathway | What it affects | Clinical note |
|------|---------|------------------|----------------|
| **MMAA** (cblA) | Adeno | B12 transport into mitochondria; stabilizes methylmalonyl-CoA mutase | Variants → elevated MMA; adeno conversion / methylmalonic acidemia. |
| **MMAB** (cblB) | Adeno | Adenosylcobalamin formation | Same. |
| **MUT** | Adeno | Methylmalonyl-CoA mutase — uses AdoCbl to convert methylmalonyl-CoA → succinyl-CoA | ~50% of isolated MMA cases; elevated MMA. |
| **MMADHC** (cblD-MMA) | Adeno | Cofactor transport/synthesis | Defects → MMA phenotype. |
| **MTR** | Methyl | Methionine synthase — homocysteine → methionine (uses methyl-B12) | Variants → homocysteine elevation; methyl conversion / methylation cycle. |
| **MTRR** | Methyl | Methionine synthase reductase — regenerates methylcobalamin for MTR | Same; MTRR variants common in hyperhomocysteinemia. |

*MTHFR (§3) supplies the folate cofactor for the MTR reaction; B12 status and MTR/MTRR affect homocysteine independently of adeno/MMA.*
